# Product FAQ

## Carretera

Carretera is positioned as a stable entry product for government and midmarket customers. It is usually sold with a lower manufacturing price and is useful when the sales team needs a predictable product with broad market coverage.

## Montana

Montana is often used in campaigns that focus on midmarket customers. It is a good product for comparing discount effects because units sold can vary significantly across countries and months.

## Paseo

Paseo is treated as a high-volume product line. When the sales team studies revenue concentration, Paseo should be reviewed together with country, segment, discount band, and profit.

## VTT

VTT is useful for analyzing whether higher sale prices translate into stronger profit. Analysts should compare gross sales, discounts, COGS, and profit before making conclusions.

## Velo

Velo is often reviewed when the team wants to compare performance across enterprise and small business segments. It can expose whether a discount program helps volume without damaging profit too much.

## Amarilla

Amarilla is usually analyzed with seasonal sales patterns. When sales are strong but profit is weak, the first things to inspect are discount band and COGS.
