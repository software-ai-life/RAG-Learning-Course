# Sales Risk Memo

## Discount Risk

Discounts can increase units sold, but they may reduce profit if the sale price is already close to COGS. Any analysis of discount performance should compare gross sales, discounts, net sales, COGS, and profit.

## Segment Risk

Different segments behave differently. Government deals may have high volume, while small business deals may be more sensitive to price. Segment-level filtering is required before making recommendations.

## Product Risk

Product-level conclusions should not be made from sales alone. A product with high gross sales can still be less attractive if discounts and COGS reduce profit.

## Time Risk

Monthly or seasonal effects can distort interpretation. Analysts should compare year, month, and country before concluding that a product or market is improving.

## Data Quality Risk

Currency fields in the financial dataset may contain symbols, commas, spaces, or accounting-style empty values. These fields should be cleaned before numeric aggregation.
